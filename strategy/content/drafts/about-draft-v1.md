## Rendered draft

> **Section 1 — Hero (H1 + subhead)**

# What Cena is.

Cena is the clinical intelligence infrastructure being built for food-as-medicine — a three-person, pre-seed company underneath the programs partner institutions already run.

> **Section 2 — Agents-first thesis (H2 + one paragraph)**

## The thesis.

Cena is built agents-first. Humans hold accountability and decisions; agents handle throughput. Internally, that's how we operate today — three people, agents as primary brainpower, work that would otherwise take a much larger team. In the program, partner clinicians hold clinical authority. Cena's agents are being built to do the throughput work underneath that authority: assemble clinical intelligence between visits, surface decision trails, and reduce the gap between what clinicians need to see and what gets to them in time.

> **Section 3 — Three people (H2 + compressed list)**

## Three people.

Aaron Sleeper, Chief Experience Officer. Vanessa Sena, CEO. Andrey Kartashov, CTO.

> **Section 4 — Accountability model (H2 + one paragraph)**

## Who holds clinical authority.

Partner clinicians retain clinical authority on every program Cena supports. Cena's substrate doesn't make clinical decisions; it captures, surfaces, and trails the decisions clinicians make.

> **Section 5 — What we're building (combined: substrate-being-built + pre-seed framing, H2 + one paragraph)**

## What we're building.

Cena is the substrate being built to capture every recommendation, every approval, every escalation as an append-only trail — so the question "who decided what, and on what basis?" is answerable years from now, not asserted in the moment. We're pre-seed, three people, and one live academic-medical-center pilot in.

## Section-by-section commentary

### Section 1 — Hero

The H1 `What Cena is.` lands verbatim per voice.md §2 About row + brief Section 1 lock. The period does the structural work: declarative, not interrogative; the headline answers itself and the page substantiates. The subhead front-loads C-017 in plain declarative form ("clinical intelligence infrastructure being built for food-as-medicine") and names the structural posture (three people, pre-seed, partner-protagonist) in one sentence. **Cena is the grammatical subject — not "we"** — to keep mission-statement register out of the first read.

- **Principle calls:** `Cap-Honest` lands the verb "being built" (honest future-tense for the *deployed-substrate* read, present-tense for the *category-claim* read — both calibrated). `Translate-Anchor` allows "infrastructure" to land literally here: About is the one cross-track recognition surface where the anchor's full noun lands without translation friction (per brief §Cross-section concerns).
- **Forbidden-pattern territory steered around:** opening with "we" (mission-statement register); founder-origin story; mission/vision framing; "passion"/"purpose-driven"; outcome percentages.
- **Alternative considered:** *"Cena is the clinical intelligence infrastructure for food-as-medicine — a three-person, pre-seed company underneath the programs partner institutions already run."* (no "being built" hedge). Cut because the present-tense version reads as a deployed-substrate claim at the page's first read — exactly the failure mode `Cap-Honest` blocks. The "being built" hedge in the subhead sets the calibrated register the rest of the page inherits.
