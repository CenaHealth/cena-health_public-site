### Section 4: What stays the partner's

**Heading:** *"What stays the partner's."* Or quieter variant per writer's call (*"Yours, throughout"* / *"Boundaries that hold"*). H2; anchor `#boundaries` per IA §6 spec implied.
**Position:** Section 4, after consolidation patterns. Per IA §6 spec ("Explicit one-section restatement of clinical authority + data custody + branding").
**Length / shape:** 1 short paragraph (3–4 sentences, ~80–100 words) OR parallel-structure card block (3 cards, one each for clinical authority / data custody / branding). Writer's call based on page rhythm; both shapes work for voice register.

**What this section must do:**
- Name three boundaries explicitly: **clinical authority** (partner-clinician retains accountability), **data custody** (Cena holds PHI on behalf of partner; partner retains custodial ownership), **branding** (partner is the protagonist in any external comms; Cena recedes as substrate).
- Frame each as a **boundary**, not a **gain**. IA §6 explicit: *"Frame as boundaries, not gains (no 'benefit' per voice.md)."* The reader is not getting something; they are keeping something.
- Use voice.md §3 inheritance "Don't say but do say" pattern: state the structural fact; let the partner name the reassurance.
- Unknown-unknown-disarming work happens here. The exec sponsor's vetting reflex is *"what's the hidden cost"*; this section names the three highest-frequency hidden costs (clinical authority shift, data ownership shift, brand co-opting) and explicitly retires each as not-a-thing.

**What this section must not do:**
- Frame any boundary as a benefit (*"You get to keep your clinical authority"* — voice.md §4 explicit "benefit" prohibition). Frame as structural fact (*"Your clinical staff retain clinical accountability"*).
- Use vendor-flattery shapes (*"We respect your authority,"* *"We honor your team's autonomy,"* *"We're here to serve your mission"*) — voice writes structure, not partnership-poetry per voice.md §2 thesis-section Don't.
- Insert outcome metrics or testimonials (voice.md §5 site-wide forbidden).
- Slip into "Cena owns" language anywhere in data-custody framing — HDG packet §3.2 explicit: custodial-not-proprietary framing is load-bearing. *"Cena holds PHI on behalf of the partner institution"* is the canonical safe phrasing; *"Cena owns patient records"* / *"records belong to Cena"* are MUST-NOT-SAY.
